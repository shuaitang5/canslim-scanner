CANSLIM cheat-sheet — gates, entry & exit rules

The 7 gates

  • C — Current quarterly EPS up ≥25% YoY, accelerating.
  • A — Annual EPS up ≥25%/yr for 3 yrs; ROE ≥17%.
  • N — New: new high / new product, breaking out of a base.
  • S — Supply & demand: volume surge on the breakout; reasonable float.
  • L — Leader, not laggard: relative strength in the top ~20–30%.
  • I — Institutional sponsorship rising (funds accumulating).
  • M — Market direction: only buy when the general market is in a confirmed uptrend.

Flags: UPPER=pass · lower=fail · ?=no data · ·=not run.

Entry (O'Neil)

  • Buy point (pivot): as price clears the base's pivot, on volume ≥40–50% above average (here: ≥1.4×).
  • Buy zone: pivot up to +5%. Past +5% = extended, don't chase.
  • Only buy when M = uptrend.

Exit — cut losses

  • Hard stop −7% to −8% below buy point. Always. No exceptions. (This scanner uses −7.5%.)
  • Sell on a break of the 50-day line on heavy volume, or climax top.

Exit — take profits & move stops

  • Take most gains at +20–25%.
  • Fast-runner exception: if up +20% within 3 weeks, hold ≥8 weeks — likely a big winner.
  • Moving the stop (where, exactly):
    • At entry: stop is −7–8% below buy point.
    • Once up ~15–20%: raise stop to break-even (your buy price) — now a free trade.
    • As it rises further: trail under the rising 50-day MA (sell if it closes below on volume), or trail roughly 10% below the recent high for fast runners.
    • O'Neil does not use a fixed trailing %; the discipline is "never let a decent gain turn into a loss" — so break-even first, then ratchet up under support.

How to read this report

CANSLIM is William O'Neil's growth-stock method (How to Make Money in Stocks). It buys fundamentally strong leaders breaking out to new highs in an uptrending market — and is equally strict about when to sell. A scan match is a screen result that fits the template, not a recommendation.

The 7 CANSLIM gates

CCurrent quarterly earnings — EPS up ≥25% YoY and accelerating.
AAnnual earnings — EPS up ≥25%/yr over 3 years; ROE ≥17%.
NNew — new high, new product/management; emerging from a base.
SSupply & demand — breakout on volume ≥40–50% above average; reasonable float.
LLeader, not laggard — relative strength in the top ~20–30% of the market.
IInstitutional sponsorship — funds own it and are adding.
MMarket direction — only buy when the general market is in a confirmed uptrend.

Gate flags: UPPER = pass · lower = fail · ? = data unavailable · · = not run. So CaNSLIM = everything passed except A.

Base patterns detected

Each candidate's chart marks the pivot (breakout trigger) from its top-confidence pattern. Patterns 1–6 are O'Neil bases; 7–8 are general TA continuation patterns.

Cup-with-HandleO'Neil's signature base. Rounded cup 12–33% deep, ≥7 weeks; right side recovers near the left peak; then a 1–2 week handle (5–15% pullback, light volume, upper half of cup). Pivot = handle high + $0.10.
High-Tight-FlagRare, explosive. Rally of 100–120%+ in 4–8 weeks (flagpole), then a tight 3–5 week consolidation giving back only 10–25%. Pivot = flag high + $0.10.
Double-Bottom (W)Two lows ~7+ weeks apart, middle peak +5–15%, second low at/below the first (undercut ideal). Pivot = middle-peak high + $0.10.
SaucerRounding bottom — a cup without a handle. Gentler and longer (8+ weeks), 8–30% deep, rounded. Valid but less common.
Ascending Triangle(General TA) Flat resistance + rising support converging to an upside breakout. Pivot = flat top + $0.10.
Flat BaseSideways ≥5 weeks within <15% range, usually after a prior run. Tight, high-probability.
Consolidation (box)(General TA) Rectangle with flat ceiling and floor (each ≤3% variation), 5–15% range, 15–40 sessions.
Three-Weeks-TightO'Neil add-on entry: 3 consecutive weekly closes within ≤1.5% of each other after an advance. Pivot = highest weekly close + $0.10.

Confidence (0–1) = how textbook the geometry is — not a probability of profit.

Entry rules (O'Neil)

  • Buy point (pivot): as price clears the base pivot, on volume ≥40–50% above average (this scanner: ≥1.4× the 50-day average).
  • Buy zone: pivot up to +5%. Beyond +5% the stock is extended — don't chase.
  • Market gate: only buy when M = confirmed uptrend.

Exit rules (O'Neil)

Cut losses — the non-negotiable rule

  • Hard stop at −7% to −8% below your buy point. Always, no exceptions. (This scanner's plan uses −7.5%.)
  • Also sell on a break of the 50-day line on heavy volume, or a climax top.

Take profits & move stops — where, exactly

  • Take most gains at +20–25%.
  • Fast-runner exception: if up +20% within 3 weeks, hold at least 8 weeks — these are often the biggest winners.
  • Stop progression:
    1. At entry → stop −7–8% below the buy point.
    2. Once up ~15–20% → raise stop to break-even (your buy price). The trade is now risk-free.
    3. As it keeps rising → trail under the rising 50-day moving average (exit on a heavy-volume close below it); for fast runners, trail roughly 10% below the recent high.
  • O'Neil uses no fixed trailing % — the rule is "never let a solid gain become a loss": break-even first, then ratchet the stop up under support as the stock advances.
